01
Getting Started
🚀
The kiosk runs in your browser — no install needed. Open the link your org administrator shared, wait a moment for it to sync with the org database, and you're in.
→
What you see on load
The kiosk opens on the Fabricate screen — a blueprint browser on the left and a detail panel on the right. The title bar shows the connection status. A blinking SYNC indicator means data is loading from the org sheet.
→
Navigation
The left sidebar has icons for each main screen: Fabricate (the anvil), Orders (the clipboard), Inventory (the person), and Ore Manifest (the table). Click any icon to switch screens.
→
No account yet?
Ask your org admin to add you under the Inventory screen. Once added you'll appear in all user dropdowns across the kiosk. You can use most features as a guest without logging in, but placing orders and managing your personal inventory requires selecting your profile.
02
Logging In
🔑
You don't need to log in to browse blueprints or view the ore manifest. But logging in links actions to your profile — your blueprints, your ores, and your orders.
→
Using the LOGIN button
Click LOGIN in the title bar (right side). A prompt appears — select your name from the list. If your account has a password, enter it when asked. Your callsign appears in the title bar once logged in.
→
Selecting your user in a dropdown
Any dropdown that says Select user works the same way — pick your name, enter your password if prompted. Your selection carries across the current session.
→
Changing your password
Go to the Inventory screen and select your profile. A CHANGE PASSWORD button is visible when your own account is loaded. Enter a new password and confirm. Your admin can also reset it directly in the sheet if needed.
PASSWORDS ARE OPTIONAL If your account has no password set, selecting your name immediately loads your profile — no prompt appears.
03
Browsing Blueprints
📋
The Fabricate screen is your main workspace. The left panel shows all available blueprints as a browsable grid. Click any card to load its full details on the right.
FILTERING BY CATEGORY
Click a category icon in the icon row to show only that item type (armor, weapons, ship components, etc.). Hover a category icon to reveal a sub-category dropdown with checkboxes for finer control. Click CLEAR FILTERS to reset.
SMART FILTERS
Affordable — shows only blueprints the org currently has enough ore to craft at quantity 1.

Owned — shows only blueprints in your personal collection (requires your user to be selected).
→
Search
Type in the search bar above the blueprint grid to filter by name. The grid updates instantly. Combine with category filters to narrow results further.
→
Quantity
Use the − / + buttons next to the quantity field to adjust how many units you want to fabricate. All material requirements and cost displays update to match. Click Max to jump to 99.
→
Autofill materials
Click Best material to auto-fill all empty slots with the highest-quality ores from the org manifest. Click Worst material for the lowest quality. Useful for quickly previewing stat ranges without manually assigning each slot.
04
Material Slots & Quality
⛏
Each blueprint requires one or more ore types. The center panel shows these as slot cards. You assign specific ore entries from the org manifest to each slot — and the quality of those ores directly affects the crafted item's stats.
→
Assigning an ore to a slot
Click the ore row inside a slot card. A picker modal opens showing only ores that match that slot's required type. Ores marked LOW STOCK don't have enough quantity for your current order size. Click an ore to assign it; click Clear slot at the top of the picker to remove the current assignment.
→
Quality sliders
Each slot has a slider from 0 to 1000 that represents ore quality. Drag it to preview how different quality levels affect the item's stats in real time. The slider does not affect the actual ore used in your order — it's a preview tool only. When you assign a real ore from the manifest, the slider snaps to that ore's recorded quality.
→
Using the Materials tab
Switch the left panel to MATERIALS to browse available ores filtered by type. Click any ore row to assign it to the matching slot. Use the type icon filters and search bar to find specific ores.

05
Reading the Stats Panel
📊
The right side of the Fabricate screen shows a live stat preview for the selected blueprint. Stats update in real time as you move quality sliders or assign ores.
BASE STATS
The values this item has at quality 0 — the minimum. Shown in the left column of the stat table. For many stats this is still a useful baseline: fabrication time, base damage, temperature range, etc.
ENHANCED STATS
The values calculated from your current slider positions or assigned ore qualities. Shown in the right column. A green delta means improvement over base; a neutral dot means the stat doesn't change with quality.
→
Fabrication time
Shown at the top of the stat panel. This is the time to fabricate one unit at the selected tier. Multiply by your quantity for the total session time.
→
Multi-slot quality
When a blueprint has more than one material slot, the final stat enhancement is calculated as a weighted average across all slots. Boosting one high-quality ore while using low-quality ores in other slots gives a partial improvement — not the full maximum.
06
Placing an Order
📦
Once you've found a blueprint you want and previewed the stats, placing an order sends a fabrication request to your org's artificers. They'll see it in the Fulfillment queue and craft it for you.

Note that the dropdown at the top of the blueprint list will let you pick from a users blueprints that they have marked as owned. If you choose to pick form any user, then choose the "Gnome Admin Master List." This will have a list of all blueprints from all users.

01
Select a blueprint
Click any blueprint card in the grid to open it in the detail view. Make sure it's the item you want — you can confirm the name at the top of the stat panel.
02
Set quantity
Use − / + to adjust how many units you need. The material cost scales with quantity — check that the org has enough stock before placing.
03
Go to Orders and create a new order
Navigate to the Orders screen and click + NEW ORDER. Alternatively, from the Fabricate screen click + ADD TO ORDER to add the current blueprint directly to a new or existing order without leaving the screen.
04
Select yourself as placer
Use the Placer dropdown to identify who the order is for. Select your name so artificers know who to deliver to. Enter your in-game callsign and RSI handle if prompted.
05
Add a note (optional)
Click ADD NOTE to add special instructions — preferred quality range, urgency, delivery location, or anything your artificer should know.
06
Place the order
Click PLACE ORDER. The order is saved instantly to the org sheet and appears in the Fulfillment queue with a New Order status.
07
Tracking Your Orders
🔍
The Orders screen shows all org fabrication requests. You can filter to see only your own orders and check their current status.
→
Finding your orders
Use the Status and Assigned dropdowns at the top to filter the queue. To see only your orders, select your callsign in the placer filter, or look for your name on the order cards. Each card shows the placer name, date, item count, and current status.
→
Order status meanings
Click a card to expand it and see the full item list and progress. The status field tells you exactly where your order is in the pipeline.

READY FOR PICKUP When your order reaches READY FOR PICKUP, your artificer has finished crafting. Arrange collection with them in-game or via Discord. Once delivered, the status moves to COMPLETE.
08
Your Inventory
🧰
The Inventory screen (person icon in the sidebar) shows your personal ore stacks and blueprint collection. Select your name in the USER dropdown to load your profile.
→
Adding ore entries
In the Materials panel on the right, click + ADD ORE or click an ore type from the type filter row. A modal opens — enter the quality (0–1000) and quantity (cSCU for ship mineables, units/rocks for hand-mined), then click COMPLETE. You can have multiple stacks of the same ore at different quality levels.
→
Editing or deleting ore entries
Hover any material entry in your list to reveal the edit (pencil) and delete (✕) buttons. Edit reopens the quality/quantity modal pre-filled. Delete removes that stack immediately.
→
My Ores filter
Toggle My Ores in the Materials panel header to switch between browsing all ore types (to add new entries) and viewing only the ore types you already have in stock.
WHY LOG YOUR ORES? Your personal material entries feed into the Affordable filter on the Fabricate screen — it checks your ores alongside the org manifest to show what you can actually build. They also appear in the Materials tab when you're the active user, letting you assign your own stock directly to blueprint slots.
09
Your Blueprint Collection
📁
The left panel of the Inventory screen shows all available blueprints. Blueprints you own are highlighted teal. Toggling ownership helps you and your artificers track what's available when planning fabrication runs.
→
Marking a blueprint as owned
With your profile loaded in the Inventory screen, click any blueprint card. It toggles between owned (teal highlight + checkmark) and not owned. The change saves to your profile in the Google Sheet automatically.
→
Filtering owned blueprints
Click My BP's in the Inventory panel header to show only the blueprints you own. Use the category icons and search bar to browse your collection.
→
Using owned status in Fabricate
On the Fabricate screen, make sure your user is selected in the User dropdown, then enable the Owned filter. The blueprint grid now shows only blueprints in your collection — useful when you know exactly what you can craft.
10
Tips & Quick Reference
⚡
A few things worth knowing that will save you time.
→
Check Affordable before ordering
Enable the Affordable filter before browsing if you want to place an order today — it instantly hides everything the org can't craft right now. If the blueprint you want isn't showing, the org is short on one or more required ores. Let your officer know what's needed.
→
Quality matters most for modified stats
Not all stats scale with quality — check the delta column in the stats panel. A green delta means higher ore quality gives a real improvement. A dot means the stat is fixed regardless of quality, so there's no benefit to requesting higher-grade ore for those slots.
→
Add notes to complex orders
If you have a preference for ore quality (e.g. "please use 700+ quality ore if available") or a specific delivery window, add it in the order notes field. Artificers see notes inside the expanded order card in the Fulfillment screen.
→
Keep your ore log current
The kiosk can only show what you have if you've logged it. After a mining run, update your ore entries in the Inventory screen. Your artificers can then see your stock when planning what to pull for an order.
→
The kiosk syncs live
All data (orders, ores, blueprint ownership) is stored in the org Google Sheet and syncs every 30 seconds. If another member just added an ore or updated an order, you'll see it within half a minute without refreshing.
QUESTIONS? If something looks wrong — a missing ore type, an order stuck in the wrong status, or your account not loading — contact your org admin. They have access to the backend sheet and can fix most issues directly.
